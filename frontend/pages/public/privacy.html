1. Introduction & Legal Basis

Georgensen Courier ("we," "us," "our," or "Georgensen") is a Canadian logistics company headquartered in Toronto, Ontario. We operate globally and handle shipment tracking, customs documentation, payment processing, and delivery management across North America and international destinations.

🍁 PIPEDA Compliance

As a Canadian entity, Georgensen collects, uses, and discloses personal information in accordance with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), which establishes rules for how private sector organizations handle personal information.

PIPEDA Requirements

We comply with PIPEDA's 10 Fair Information Principles, including obtaining consent before collecting personal information, limiting use to identified purposes, and providing access to personal records upon request.

Quebec Law 25 (Bill 64)

For customers in Quebec, we also comply with Law 25 (amendments to Quebec's Personal Information Protection Law, effective 2024), which strengthens privacy protections and increases our obligations around consent, data minimization, and breach notification.

International Frameworks

For customers and partners in the EU, we respect GDPR principles. For US-based operations, we follow relevant state privacy laws including CCPA (California) and others.

2. Information Collection & Types of Data

We collect personal information necessary to provide logistics services, calculate shipping quotes, facilitate international customs clearance, and enable real-time tracking.

Categories of Personal Information We Collect:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, company name
  • Address Information: Pickup location (postal code, street address, city, province/state, country)
  • Delivery Information: Destination address, recipient contact, special delivery instructions
  • Package Details: Description, weight (kg), dimensions (cm), declared value, item type (documents, electronics, fragile, perishable, hazmat)
  • Tracking Data: Real-time GPS location, delivery timestamps, proof of delivery signatures/photos
  • Payment & Billing: Transaction amounts, invoice data (credit card details handled by PCI-DSS compliant processors only, not stored by us)
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, device ID, operating system, pages visited, time on site
  • Customs Information: Tariff classification, HS codes, origin of goods, export/import documentation

Location Data (GPS & Tracking)

If you enable tracking via our mobile app or web platform, we collect your device's GPS location to:

  • Provide real-time shipment tracking with location accuracy
  • Display location on interactive maps (province/state level)
  • Estimate delivery timeframes based on geolocation
  • Prevent fraud and unauthorized package access

Note: Location data is deleted within 30 days of delivery unless you retain it for dispute resolution.

3. Use of Data (Purposes & Logistics-Specific Uses)

We collect and use personal information for the following logistics-specific purposes:

Core Service Delivery:

  • Calculate regional shipping quotes with accurate postal code validation
  • Verify pickup and delivery addresses for accuracy
  • Generate customs documentation and HS code classification
  • Facilitate cross-border clearance (CBSA coordination)
  • Provide real-time tracking and delivery notifications
  • Coordinate with third-party carriers (FedEx, UPS)

Regional Tax Calculation:

  • Determine provincial tax rates (ON HST 13%, QC 14.975%, BC 12%, AB 5%, etc.)
  • Calculate fuel surcharges based on destination zones
  • Apply customs broker fees ($50 CAD for international shipments)

Billing & Payment:

  • Process quote requests and generate invoices
  • Verify payment authorization
  • Manage refunds and dispute resolution

Customer Support & Communication:

  • Respond to support inquiries and contact form submissions
  • Send delivery status updates (email, SMS)
  • Notify of exceptions (delays, customs holds, exceptions)

Security & Fraud Prevention:

  • Detect fraudulent transactions using IP address and device ID analysis
  • Prevent unauthorized access to tracking information
  • Monitor for suspicious patterns in shipment requests

Legal & Compliance:

  • Comply with CBSA import/export regulations
  • Respond to legal requests from customs authorities
  • Maintain records for regulatory audits

4. Data Residency & International Transfers

As a global logistics company, your personal information may be stored, processed, or transferred outside of Canada to facilitate international shipments and carrier coordination.

Data Storage Locations:

  • Primary: Canada (AWS Toronto region) - Core database and tracking records
  • Backup: US (AWS N. Virginia) - Disaster recovery & redundancy
  • Processing: Global carrier systems (FedEx, UPS) - Real-time tracking updates

Cross-Border Data Flows:

⚠️ International Transfers

When your shipment crosses borders (US, EU, Asia), your tracking information, address, and package details are shared with customs authorities, border agents, and third-party carriers operating in those jurisdictions. These transfers are necessary to complete delivery and comply with international trade regulations.

Why International Transfer is Necessary:

  • CBSA (Canada Border Services Agency) clearance requires sharing pickup/delivery addresses and package contents
  • US CBP (Customs & Border Protection) requires advance cargo information for entry into the United States
  • FedEx/UPS operate global networks requiring access to tracking and address data
  • To provide real-time tracking with accurate GPS coordinates across multiple countries

Legal Frameworks Protecting International Transfers:

  • US-Canada Mutual Legal Assistance Treaty
  • Canadian-US Regulatory Cooperation Agreement
  • GDPR Standard Contractual Clauses (for EU shipments)

Your Consent: By using our services to ship internationally, you consent to these international data transfers. You can limit international shipping by restricting shipments to Canadian-only addresses.

5. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies, local storage, and similar technologies to enhance your experience and provide reliable tracking functionality.

Types of Tracking Technologies:

  • Essential Cookies: Session management, security, form submission (required for site functionality)
  • Preference Cookies: Language selection (EN/FR toggle stored in localStorage), currency preference (CAD/USD)
  • Tracking Cookies: Google Analytics, shipment history, quote calculation data
  • Third-Party Cookies: Payment processors (Stripe), carrier APIs (FedEx, UPS)

Technical Data Collected via Tracking:

  • IP address (used for geolocation and fraud detection)
  • Device ID and hardware identifiers (Apple IDFA, Android AAID)
  • Browser fingerprint (user-agent, supported fonts, plugins)
  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Click patterns and form completions
  • Referrer information (where you came from)

Purpose of Technical Tracking:

  • Prevent fraud and unauthorized access to tracking information
  • Detect bot activity and DDoS attacks
  • Improve user interface based on usage patterns
  • Provide seamless quote calculations and tracking updates

Cookie Control:

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Disable all cookies (may limit functionality)
  • Accept only essential cookies
  • Clear cookies after each session
  • Use private/incognito browsing

Note: Disabling cookies may prevent features like real-time tracking, quote saving, and bilingual language selection from working properly.

6. Third-Party Sharing & Disclosure

We share personal information with trusted partners only when necessary to deliver services or comply with legal obligations.

Third-Party Categories:

Customs & Government Authorities
  • CBSA: Sender/recipient name, address, phone, package contents, value (required for import/export clearance)
  • US CBP: Advanced cargo information for US-bound shipments
  • Police/Law Enforcement: Information disclosed upon court order or legal request
Third-Party Carriers
  • FedEx: Full tracking data (pickup, delivery, GPS coordinates, signatures)
  • UPS: Full tracking data and customs documentation
  • Regional Couriers: Address, tracking number, package details
Service Providers
  • Payment Processors: Stripe (payment transaction data only; credit card details never stored by us)
  • Email Providers: SendGrid (delivery notifications and receipts)
  • Analytics: Google Analytics (anonymized usage data)
Legal Basis for Sharing
  • PIPEDA: Service delivery and legal compliance
  • Cross-border trade: Customs regulations mandate information sharing
  • Court orders or regulatory requests
  • Your explicit consent (e.g., contact form to support team)

Important: We do NOT sell your personal information to marketing agencies, data brokers, or advertisers. Third-party sharing is limited to what is necessary for service delivery and legal compliance.

7. Your Rights & Choices

Under PIPEDA and applicable privacy laws, you have the following rights related to your personal information:

Right to Access

You can request a copy of all personal information we hold about you. We will provide this within 30 days of your request (as required by PIPEDA).

Right to Correction/Rectification

If your information is inaccurate or incomplete, you can request correction (e.g., update your address, fix your phone number).

Right to Deletion

You can request deletion of your personal information, except where we are required to retain it for:

  • Customs/regulatory compliance (7 years for export records)
  • Legal disputes or insurance claims (3 years)
  • Fraud investigation

Right to Opt-Out

You can opt-out of non-essential communications (marketing emails, promotional offers). You can do this by clicking "Unsubscribe" in any email or via your account settings. You cannot opt-out of transactional emails (delivery notifications, support responses).

Right to Data Portability

You can request your personal information in a structured, commonly used format (CSV, PDF) so you can transfer it to another service provider.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you can file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC).

How to Exercise Your Rights

Email privacy@georgensen.ca with your request. Include:

  • Your full name and email address
  • Specific request (access, correction, deletion, opt-out)
  • Relevant shipment tracking number (if applicable)
  • Proof of identity for verification

Response Timeline: We will respond within 30 days (PIPEDA requirement). Complex requests may take up to 60 days.

8. Security of Your Information

We implement technical, organizational, and administrative safeguards to protect personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, and disclosure.

Security Measures:

  • Encryption in Transit: TLS 1.3 for all website/app connections (https://)
  • Encryption at Rest: AES-256 encryption for database records containing sensitive information
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) - employees access only necessary data
  • Payment Security: PCI-DSS compliance through Stripe (credit card data never stored locally)
  • Firewalls & Intrusion Detection: AWS WAF and CloudTrail monitoring
  • Regular Audits: Quarterly security assessments and penetration testing
  • Employee Training: PIPEDA and data privacy training for all staff

Breach Notification

If we discover a breach of personal information that creates a real risk of significant harm, we will notify:

  • Affected individuals (within 30 days)
  • Office of the Privacy Commissioner (if significant)
  • Relevant media outlets (if publicly reported)

Limitation: No security system is 100% secure. While we use industry-standard protections, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Data Retention & Deletion

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the identified purposes or as required by law.

Retention Schedule:

  • Active Shipments: 90 days after delivery
  • Billing/Invoice Records: 7 years (CRA requirement for businesses)
  • Customs Documentation: 7 years (CBSA requirement)
  • Customer Support Tickets: 3 years
  • Tracking History: 30 days (GPS data) / 1 year (delivery records)
  • Login Sessions & Cookies: Automatically deleted after 24 hours of inactivity
  • Payment Data: Not retained (handled by Stripe only)
  • Failed Login Attempts: 90 days for fraud investigation

Account Deletion

You can request account deletion at any time. We will delete all associated personal information except where retention is required by law (invoices, customs records, dispute resolution).
