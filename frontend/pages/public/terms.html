4. Customs, Duties & Taxes

International shipments are subject to customs duties, import taxes, and brokerage fees in destination countries. The Receiver (Importer of Record) is responsible for all applicable customs charges.

Canadian Outbound Shipments

  • Sender must provide accurate Harmonized System (HS) codes for items
  • Sender responsible for customs export documentation
  • CBSA (Canada Border Services Agency) clearance required before dispatch

International Inbound Shipments to Canada

  • Receiver responsible for import duties and HST/GST on item value
  • Receiver may pay brokerage fees ($50+ CAD) for customs clearance
  • CBSA may delay shipments for inspection (1-5 business days)
  • No liability for customs delays beyond Georgensen's control

US-Bound Shipments

  • Advance cargo information required for US CBP (Customs & Border Protection)
  • Receiver responsible for US import duties and state sales tax
  • US brokerage fees apply (typically $25-75 USD per shipment)

Provincial Tax Rates (Canada)

Georgensen calculates provincial taxes based on destination postal code:

  • Ontario (ON): 13% HST
  • Quebec (QC): 14.975% (5% GST + 9.975% PST)
  • British Columbia (BC): 12% (5% GST + 7% PST)
  • Alberta (AB): 5% GST only
  • Manitoba (MB): 13% (5% GST + 8% PST)
  • Saskatchewan (SK): 11% (5% GST + 6% PST)
  • Nova Scotia (NS): 15% HST
  • New Brunswick (NB): 15% HST
  • Prince Edward Island (PE): 15% HST
  • Newfoundland & Labrador (NL): 15% HST

6. International Shipping Liability & Carrier Limits

Georgensen acts as a logistics coordinator, not the direct carrier. Liability for loss or damage is governed by international convention and third-party carrier agreements.

Montreal Convention (International Air Carriage)

For international air shipments, liability is governed by the Montreal Convention (1999), which limits carrier liability to approximately $20 USD per kg or the declared value, whichever is less. This applies to FedEx International and UPS International Express services.

Standard Shipping Liability (Domestic)

For domestic Canadian shipments, liability is limited to the shipping fee paid unless Extended Coverage is purchased (see Section 7).

Carrier-Specific Terms

  • FedEx: Responsibility begins at pickup and ends at delivery. Liability subject to FedEx terms of service and Montreal Convention for international.
  • UPS: Responsibility begins at pickup and ends at delivery. Liability subject to UPS terms of service and Montreal Convention for international.
  • Regional Couriers: Liability per courier agreement; typically limited to $100-500 CAD unless Extended Coverage purchased.

Disclaimers

  • Georgensen is NOT liable for loss, theft, or damage to packages in carrier possession
  • Georgensen is NOT liable for delays caused by customs inspections, weather, or international port congestion
  • Georgensen is NOT liable for indirect damages (lost business, lost profits, consequential damages)
ℹ️ Important for High-Value Shipments

If you are shipping items worth more than $1,000 CAD, we strongly recommend purchasing Extended Coverage (insurance) to protect against loss or damage.

7. Insurance & Extended Coverage

Due to liability limits imposed by international convention and carriers, Georgensen offers optional Extended Coverage (insurance) to protect high-value shipments.

Standard Shipping (No Insurance)

  • Liability limited to shipping fee ($8-250 CAD)
  • Montreal Convention limits apply ($20 USD per kg)
  • No coverage for theft, weather delays, or customs hold-ups
  • Recommended for items under $500 CAD

Extended Coverage (Optional Insurance)

  • Coverage up to declared shipment value (90-100% based on deductible)
  • Covers loss, damage, theft, and weather-related delays
  • Premium: 1-5% of shipment value depending on risk category
  • Deductible: $50-200 CAD per claim
  • Recommended for items over $500 CAD

Coverage Exclusions

Extended Coverage does NOT cover:

  • Prohibited items or illegal shipments
  • Items with insufficient or incorrect packaging
  • Misrepresented item values or contents
  • Claims not filed within 30 days of delivery date
  • Consequential damages or business losses

Filing a Claim

To file an insurance claim:

  • Report issue within 48 hours of delivery to support@georgensen.ca
  • Provide tracking number, photos of damage/packaging, and itemized list of contents
  • Claims adjudicated within 10-15 business days
  • Payment issued to original shipper upon approval

Georgensen is not liable for delays or failures caused by events beyond reasonable control:

Qualifying Events

  • Extreme weather conditions (snowstorms, ice storms blocking roads)
  • War, terrorism, civil unrest, or government actions
  • Customs delays and inspections (1-14 days typical)
  • International port congestion or labor strikes

Customs Hold-Ups (Expected)

Customs delays are NOT refundable and are expected for international shipments. Typical timelines:

  • US CBP: 1-2 days (expedited) to 5-10 days (inspected)

Communication During Delays

If a shipment experiences a force majeure event:

  • We will provide status update within 24 hours
